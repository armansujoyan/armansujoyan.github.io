Most common encryption algorithm.

RSA (Rivest–Shamir–Adleman)

The problem was creating a one way function that cannot be inverted and they did it!

They actually take the exponent of a number and then modulo it with prime number.

More detailed.

  • Choose two distinct prime numbers p and q.
  • Compute n = pq.
    • n is used as the modulus for both the public and private keys. Its length, usually expressed in bits, is the key length.
  • Compute λ(n) = lcm(λ(p), λ(q)) = lcm(p − 1, q − 1). This number is kept private.
  • Choose an integer e such that 1 < e < λ(n) and gcd(e, λ(n)) = 1; i.e., e and λ(n) are coprime.
  • Determine d as d ≡ e−1 (mod λ(n)); i.e., d is the modular multiplicative inverse of e (modulo λ(n))
